A history in two buildings

The Central Library began in a small room of the Institute's old building, Shahid Bhavan, in 1951, and moved into its present premises in 1956. Over seven decades it has grown from a single room into one of the largest technical libraries on the continent — adapting, generation after generation, to whatever computers, microelectronics, and communication technology asked of it next.

It now serves undergraduates, postgraduates, research scholars, faculty, and staff across every department, centre, and school on campus — not as an archive to be visited, but as working infrastructure for research and study, open nearly around the clock.

04

Digital library & e-resources

NDLI

National Digital Library of India

A virtual repository of learning resources sponsored by the Ministry of Education, offering federated search across a vast body of academic content — with an active NDLI-Club on campus.

IDR

Institutional Digital Repository

Built on DSpace, IIT Kharagpur was the first of the eight IIT libraries to launch an institutional repository, organised by department, centre, and school.

INDEST

INDEST-AICTE Consortium

Central Library is a core member, providing online access to major full-text science and engineering databases and abstracting services.

AR/VR

AR/VR Facility

Access to virtual-reality educational content — videos, simulations, and interactive activities — with tools for students to build and test their own VR content.

AVL

Audio Visual Lounge

An immersive space for presentations and video conferencing, equipped with touch-screen monitors and dedicated computers.

ETD

Electronic Thesis & Dissertation

Manages the submission of PhD theses into the Central Library, run out of the Technical Processing Section.

05

Library sections & services

§1

Circulation Section

Handles the day-to-day issue and return of books for every member of the Institute.

§2

Acquisition Section

Collects book requests from faculty and departments and places orders with suppliers.

§3

Technical Processing

Classifies and catalogues every new acquisition, bridging the gap between acquisition and circulation.

§4

Document Delivery (DDS)

Sources soft copies of book chapters or journal articles on request and delivers them by email.

§5

OPAC

The online public access catalogue for searching holdings, viewing issued books and due dates, checking fines, and placing reservations.

§6

Rare & Digitised Books

A number of unique printed works have been digitised for online access, available on request for academic and research use.

06

Membership & circulation

All IIT Kharagpur employees, students, and faculty are eligible for library membership. Loan limits and durations vary by category — undergraduates, postgraduates, research scholars, staff, and faculty each carry a different entitlement.

Books are shelved by the Dewey Decimal Classification. Hall 1 carries every category; the other halls each hold a defined subset, as set out under Reading Halls above.
